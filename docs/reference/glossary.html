3.3. GlossaryÂ¶

COL_GEN
The fraction of a morphology in which hoppers can be collected or generated. A COL_GEN of 0.05 implies that the bottom 5% of the film (along the Z-axis) can generate hoppers, and the top 5% of the film can collect them.
DE
The difference in site energies.
J
The transfer integral.
collector
Vertices on which hoppers can be collected. See also section Vertex types.
edge file
A description of the connections between vertices in the film.
energetic driving force
The difference between two site energies.
generator
Vertices on which hoppers can be generated. See also section Vertex types.
glob pattern
The standard way in which filenames are expanded on UNIX type systems. For example, massive_mobilities*out would match, massive_mobilities_0.out, massive_mobilities_1.out, and massive_mobilities_I_doubt.
hopper
A hole, electron, or exciton that can move between vertices.
run
An individual ‘run’ of the Monte Carlo code which ends, for examples, when the convergence criteria have been met, or maxTime exceeded. To ensure good sampling, a simulation should average results over many runs.
sim file
All of the parameters needed to define the running of the simulation.
site Energy
The energy of a hopper when on a particular vertex. ToFeT considers the site energy to consist of a static part, which is due to identity of individual molecules and their packing, and a dynamic part, which is due to the Coulombic interaction between hoppers.
transfer Integral
The electronic coupling between different charge transport vertices.
vertex
A point within the film morphology. This generally refers to a single charge transport unit such as the centre of a small molecule, or a conjugated segment of a polymer.
xyz file
The coordinates of each vertex in the film, it’s opto-electronic characteristics and, optionally, it’s site energy.
